My Time in the Classroom and Technology

My time in the classroom really influenced my perspective on technology.

Teenagers generally love playing with technology, so they taught me a thing or two about it. They truly are very bright and pick up new ideas very quickly if given the chance to explore; when technology was involved, I didn't even have to ask them to explore!

As we try to teach 21st century learners and our future labor force, it is VITAL that we teach students the technological skills they need to be successful in our current market. Learning how to use their cell phones and how to navigate the Internet are necessary, but it is only the beginning.

It is of the utmost importance that we all have digital literacy! Not all of us have to be web designers or software engineers, but we all need to have some background on these topics to make ourselve competitive in the workforce.

A college graduate once shared a story that has stuck with me for years-- he was hired to do data entry. As a STEM major, he couldn't keep up with the fast paced typing of the English majors that were working for the company. Instead of trying to out-type them, he used his coding knowledge. He wrote a script that did his data entry job for him. Within a month or so, the others were let go, he was asked to get other STEM majors on board, and he built a great relationship with his boss that continues to have a positive effect on his career.

I repeat and highlight: it is VITAL that we teach students the technological skills they need to be successful in our current market. Possessing some coding knowledge is something that no one can afford to lack. STEM major or not, we all need to have this type of digital literacy.
